The channels of meaning

Meaning isn't just carried by words.

It travels through every signal an organization sends—intentionally and unintentionally. The gap between intent and interpretation is where organizations lose momentum.

01

What is said

The explicit communication, the language chosen, the words in the email or the all-hands.

02

How it's said

Tone, warmth or distance, urgency or calm. The feeling of a message before its content is processed.

03

What is done

Behaviors and actions that either confirm or contradict the words. The gap where trust is made or lost.

04

Who was involved

Who was in the room. Who was consulted. Who shaped the decision—and who didn't.

05

When it happened

Timing sends its own signal. The same message lands differently in different organizational moments.

06

What wasn't said

Silence, omission, the thing everyone noticed wasn't addressed. Often the loudest signal of all.

The Three-Part Meaning Landscape

Every organization has meaning that is working well, meaning sitting untapped, and meaning causing drift. Most leaders can only see one of the three—and it's rarely the most important one.

Working Well

Meaning is landing as intended. Alignment is strong. People are moving together—and you can see it in execution, in retention, in culture that holds under pressure. When you know where meaning lands well, you can intentionally repeat and scale those conditions.

Sitting Untapped

Latent alignment that isn't yet activated. Untapped performance. Organizations rarely know it's there—which means they rarely reach for it. Neutral isn't neutral; it's potential that isn't working for you yet.

Causing Drift

Interpretation is diverging from intent. Meaning is fracturing quietly—before it becomes visible as a problem, before results start slipping, before the question becomes unanswerable. Caught early, drift is workable. Ignored, it compounds.
